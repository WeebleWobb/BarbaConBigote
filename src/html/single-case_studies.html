Website Redesign.

Creating Empathy and Emotional Connections.

One of the first challenges I was presented during my employment at Synchrony Group was the redesign of the company website. The website had already gone through multiple attempts on a redesign but non that were ever approved or made it past the initial design phase. The challenge wasn’t just to redesign a company website but to understand why there were so many failed attempts, what were the pain points of the process, and how can we create a process that tackles the main objective.

01.

Discovery and User Experience

During further investigation of the project history, it became clear why so many attempts failed. There was an absence of Discovery and User Experience. Instead of jumping straight to design comps, I took a step back and took a deeper dive at what the primary goal(s) of the site should be, who are intended users were, what would be there expected interaction, and why was a redesign ultimately needed?

Exercises such as: Stakeholder interviews, Persona Maps, User Flows, Site Maps, and Wireframes assisted in painting a clear direction for the project; removing all subjectivity and allowing for a research based approach.

Sitemap

Once we locked who our users were, what the primary and secondary goals were, and mapping out each target users user flow, we were able to identify what pages, content, and features would be needed to guide the user towards our intended goals. The discovery and UX phase allowed us to holistically map out the experience with the user in mind and create a sitemap that allows for a seamless content flow.

Wireframes

With the complete site structure and user flow identified, we could begin the overall page structure. The goals was not just to layout the blueprint for each page but to create an overall content flow that would continue to guide the user to the primary and secondary goals.

The challenge was to avoid a design first mentality and instead focus on the user. How they would engage with the site, what content would we provide, what call to actions would we present to guide the user to our primary goals, how can we structure the content to create a complete story

We were not just redesigning a website but recreating an experience.

02.

Design and Development

Challenging ourselves to avoid a Design First mentality, we were able to objectively create the intended experience. This allowed us to focus on the design aesthetic with out having to questions each design aesthetic. Our design decisions would continue to be based around the user and the experience.

We wanted the design phase to continue to support the users experience and intended goal. By using photography and digestible content, we were able to create an aesthetic that reflected empathy. The goal was to paint an accurate picture of the companies culture, experience, professionalism, and overall make an emotional connection.
